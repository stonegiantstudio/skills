# Copywriting Formulas & Frameworks

Proven structures for headlines, body copy, and complete pieces.

---

## Copy Frameworks

### AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)

The classic structure. Works for virtually any length.

| Stage         | Goal                               | Techniques                           |
| ------------- | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| **Attention** | Stop the scroll, interrupt pattern | Bold claim, question, shocking stat  |
| **Interest**  | Make them want to know more        | Benefits, story, relevance           |
| **Desire**    | Make them want it                  | Social proof, visualization, urgency |
| **Action**    | Tell them exactly what to do       | Clear CTA, remove friction           |

---

### PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)

Best for problem-aware audiences. Simple and brutal.

1. **Problem:** Name their pain clearly
2. **Agitate:** Twist the knife—what happens if unsolved?
3. **Solution:** Your product/offer as the answer

*Example:*

> **Problem:** Your email list isn't growing.
> **Agitate:** Every day without subscribers is money left on the table. While competitors build audiences, you're invisible.
> **Solution:** [Product] adds 100+ subscribers per week on autopilot.

---

### PASO (Problem → Agitate → Solution → Outcome)

PAS with a positive ending—shows the after state.

Add: **Outcome:** Paint the picture of success after using the solution.

---

### 4 Ps (Picture → Promise → Prove → Push)

1. **Picture:** Paint a vivid image of the outcome
2. **Promise:** What you'll deliver
3. **Prove:** Evidence, testimonials, data
4. **Push:** CTA with urgency

---

### Before-After-Bridge (BAB)

1. **Before:** Their current painful situation
2. **After:** The transformed future
3. **Bridge:** Your product as the path from Before to After

*Example:*

> **Before:** You're drowning in spreadsheets, working weekends.
> **After:** Imagine closing your laptop at 5pm, reports done automatically.
> **Bridge:** [Product] automates your entire reporting workflow.

---

### The 4 U's (Headlines)

Every headline should have at least one, ideally all:

| U                  | Description         | Example                                   |
| ------------------ | ------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| **Useful**         | Benefits the reader | "How to Save 3 Hours a Week"              |
| **Urgent**         | Time-sensitive      | "Last Day: 50% Off Everything"            |
| **Unique**         | Different, novel    | "The Backwards Method to Learning Guitar" |
| **Ultra-specific** | Concrete details    | "How I Made $11,847 in 30 Days"           |

## Headline Formulas

### Benefit-Driven Headlines

- **How to [Achieve Desired Result]**
  "How to Win Friends and Influence People"

- **[Number] Ways to [Achieve Benefit]**
  "7 Ways to Double Your Productivity This Week"

- **The Secret of [Achieving Something Desirable]**
  "The Secret of Making People Like You"

- **[Do Something] Like [Expert/Aspirational Figure]**
  "Write Emails Like a 7-Figure Copywriter"

---

### Curiosity Headlines

- **Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong**
  "Why Everything You Know About Dieting Is Wrong"

- **What [Authority/Group] Doesn't Want You to Know About [Topic]**
  "What Wall Street Doesn't Want You to Know About Retirement"

- **The [Adjective] [Thing] That [Surprising Result]**
  "The Lazy Way to Double Your Income"

---

### News Headlines

- **Introducing [New Thing]**
  "Introducing the First AI That Writes Your Emails"

- **Finally, [Solution to Long-Standing Problem]**
  "Finally, a Mattress That Actually Helps You Sleep"

---

### Caples' Greatest Hits Formulas

John Caples tested thousands of headlines. These patterns consistently won:

1. **They Laughed When [I Did X]—But When [Result]!**
   "They Laughed When I Sat Down at the Piano—But When I Started to Play!"

2. **Do You Make These Mistakes in [Area]?**
   "Do You Make These Mistakes in English?"

3. **How [Surprising/Unlikely Person] [Achieved Result]**
   "How a Strange Accident Saved Me From Baldness"

4. **Give Me [Small Commitment] and I'll [Big Promise]**
   "Give Me 5 Days and I'll Give You a Magnetic Personality"

5. **Who Else Wants [Desirable Thing]?**
   "Who Else Wants a Hollywood Smile?"

### Power Words for Headlines

**Urgency:** Now, Today, Hurry, Limited, Last chance, Deadline
**Exclusivity:** Secret, Insider, Private, Members-only, Hidden
**Value:** Free, Bonus, Save, Discount, Bargain, Value
**Transformation:** Transform, Breakthrough, Revolutionary, Discover
**Authority:** Proven, Guaranteed, Research-backed, Expert, Official
**Emotion:** Love, Fear, Hate, Worried, Embarrassed, Proud

## Email Subject Line Formulas

Short, intriguing, personal. Subject lines are headlines optimized for inbox scanning.

- **[First Name], quick question**
- **Re: Your [thing they care about]**
- **Don't make this mistake**
- **[Number] [things] you're doing wrong**
- **I owe you an apology**
- **This made me think of you**
- **Weird trick for [desired outcome]**
- **The truth about [controversial topic]**

## CTA Formulas

### Button Text

Action-oriented, specific, low-friction:

| Weak       | Strong               |
| ---------- | -------------------- |
| Submit     | Get My Free Guide    |
| Click Here | Start My Free Trial  |
| Buy        | Yes, I Want This     |
| Learn More | Show Me How It Works |
| Sign Up    | Join 10,000+ Members |

### CTA Surrounding Copy

- **Remove risk:** "Start your free trial—cancel anytime"
- **Add urgency:** "Only 3 spots left at this price"
- **Stack value:** "Get instant access to [X], [Y], and [Z]"
- **Repeat benefit:** "Start getting [result] today"

## Landing Page Formula

### Above the Fold (Hero Section)

1. **Headline:** Main benefit or big idea
2. **Subhead:** Secondary benefit or clarification
3. **Visual:** Product image, demo, or emotional imagery
4. **CTA:** Primary action button
5. **Social proof snippet:** "Trusted by 10,000+ companies"

### Body Structure

1. **Problem section:** Identify and agitate the pain
2. **Solution section:** Introduce your approach
3. **Benefits section:** 3-5 key benefits with supporting details
4. **How it works:** Simple 3-step process
5. **Social proof:** Testimonials, logos, case studies
6. **FAQ:** Handle objections
7. **Final CTA:** Recap offer, repeat CTA

## Sales Letter Structure (Long-Form)

Based on Gary Halbert and Eugene Schwartz principles:

1. **Pre-head:** Qualifier or curiosity hook
2. **Headline:** Big promise or benefit
3. **Deck copy:** Expands headline, adds specificity
4. **Opening:** Story or hook that creates identification
5. **Problem:** Describe their situation
6. **Agitation:** What happens if they don't solve it
7. **Solution:** Your unique mechanism/approach
8. **Benefits:** What they get (outcomes, not features)
9. **Proof:** Testimonials, case studies, data
10. **Offer:** Exactly what's included
11. **Bonuses:** Added value
12. **Price justification:** Why it's worth more
13. **Guarantee:** Risk reversal
14. **Scarcity/Urgency:** Why act now
15. **CTA:** Clear instruction to buy
16. **P.S.:** Restate key benefit, urgency, or guarantee

*Note: P.S. lines are often read first—make them count.*
